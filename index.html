About the Project

Flood control projects promise safety and resilience. But their implementation can bring complex trade-offs—social displacement, ecological damage, budget overruns, and local safety hazards. This project documents both the official plans and the consequences left in the shadows.

Our Goals

  • Document community experiences and testimonies.
  • Collect and present objective research and data.
  • Promote transparency and better planning.
  • Encourage solutions that balance safety and livelihoods.

What a Typical Flood Control Project Includes

  • Drainage systems and pumping stations
  • River widening and channelization
  • Dikes, levees, and retaining walls
  • Slope protection and slope regrading

Each measure can reduce flood risk locally — but can also shift impacts to other areas or harm ecosystems when poorly planned.

Environmental Impact

River Ecosystems

Channelization and river widening alter habitats and migration routes for fish and aquatic life. Natural floodplains often act as sponges — removing them increases flow speed and downstream risks.

Soil, Erosion, and Sedimentation

Unplanned bank reinforcement and excavation can destabilize nearby slopes and increase sediment downstream, harming water quality and fisheries.

Vegetation Loss

Removal of riparian trees and mangroves reduces biodiversity and increases local heat and erosion.

Risk Transfer

Improvements in one area can push flood risk to adjacent barangays if flow paths aren't carefully modelled.

Issues & Controversies

Budget Transparency

We track reported budgets, contractor changes, and delays. Documented overruns and unclear procurement records raise questions about accountability.

Construction Quality

Reports of substandard materials, unfinished sections, and visible structural issues have been raised by residents and inspectors.

Public Consultation

Affected communities often report insufficient consultation. We encourage meaningful, documented engagement before construction begins.

Environmental Compliance

We compile environmental impact statements and note where compliance documentation is missing or inaccessible to the public.

Viewpoints & Solutions

Government

  • Protection from natural disasters: Essential to prevent loss of lives and property.
  • Economic benefits: Encourages investment, development, and infrastructure growth.
  • Long-term cost savings: Prevention is cheaper than repeated disaster recovery.

Environmentalists

  • Ecosystem disruption: Alters river flow, wetlands, and fish habitats.
  • False sense of security: Can lead people to settle in vulnerable areas.
  • Climate resilience concerns: Some projects may fail under future climate realities.

Local Community

  • Support for protection: Reduced flooding and improved safety welcomed.
  • Concerns about displacement: Fear losing homes or livelihoods.
  • Distrust due to transparency issues: Budget misuse or questionable contractors create tension.
  • Impact on daily life: Construction noise, traffic, temporary livelihood disruptions.

Economists

  • High project cost: Budget allocations raise prioritization questions.
  • Risk of corruption: Contracts prone to overpricing or kickbacks.
  • Cost-benefit concerns: Benefits may not justify public spending.
  • Maintenance burden: Long-term upkeep may not be sustained.

Planners & Engineers

  • Technical necessity: Flood control sometimes unavoidable in dense cities.
  • Limitations of structures: Dikes or channels alone cannot prevent all flooding.
  • Risk of engineering oversights: Poor design may worsen flooding elsewhere.

Human Rights & Social Justice Groups

  • Unequal impact on the poor: Low-income households suffer most from relocation.
  • Lack of consultation: Communities often feel unheard.
  • Right to safe housing and livelihood: Emphasizes humane relocation, fair compensation, and long-term support.

Media & Public

  • Mixed reactions: Project seen as necessary by some, political tool by others.
  • Highlight controversies: Delays, corruption allegations, or environmental damage.
  • Public pressure for accountability: Transparency in budgets, timelines, and compliance.
